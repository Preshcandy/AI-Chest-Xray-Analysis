AI-Assisted Chest X-Ray Analysis for Radiographic Diagnosis

Project Overview
This project explores the application of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in assisting radiographers and radiologists in the interpretation of chest radiographs (CXR). The study focuses on evaluating how deep learning models can support diagnostic workflows by detecting common thoracic pathologies and improving diagnostic efficiency.
The research forms part of an ongoing academic investigation into AI-enhanced medical image analysis and the evolving role of radiographers in medical informatics.

Problem Statement
Chest X-ray interpretation remains one of the most commonly performed diagnostic imaging tasks worldwide. However, interpretation can be time-consuming and subject to human variability.
Artificial intelligence has emerged as a promising tool to:
°Support clinical decision-making
°Reduce diagnostic workload
°Improve detection of subtle abnormalities
This project investigates how AI-assisted analysis compares with traditional radiologist interpretation in terms of diagnostic precision and workflow support.

Objectives
•Evaluate the diagnostic performance of AI systems in chest X-ray interpretation.
•Conduct a systematic review and proportion meta-analysis comparing AI models with human radiologists.
•Identify gaps in current AI diagnostic models used in radiology.
•Explore opportunities for integrating AI into radiographic practice.

Methodology
1. The project adopts a systematic research approach including:
Literature search across multiple medical databases.
2. Selection of peer-reviewed studies focusing on AI-based chest radiograph interpretation.
3. Data extraction on diagnostic performance metrics such as:
   °Sensitivity
   °Specificity
   °Accuracy
4. Statistical comparison between AI models and radiologist performance.
All references and documentation follow Harvard referencing style.
